Common Questions

Parents Often Ask Us...

Is the scholarship test difficult? ▼
No — the assessment is a simple test based on your child's current class level. It's designed to understand what the child already knows, not to trick or fail them. Any sincere, motivated student can do well.
Can scholarship students really keep up with paid students? ▼
Absolutely. All our students — scholarship and non-scholarship — study in the same batches, with the same teachers, and the same study material. There is no difference in the quality of teaching or attention given.
What documents do we need for the scholarship application? ▼
For the initial enquiry, you only need to call or WhatsApp us. When you come to the institute, bring your child's recent school report card or mark sheet. For income-based assessment, a simple self-declaration is sufficient.
How many scholarship seats are available per batch? ▼
We keep very limited seats — typically 2 to 4 scholarship students per batch. This is because we maintain small batch sizes and want every student to get adequate attention. First come, first served after the assessment.
Is this scholarship only for defence courses or school batches too? ▼
Scholarships are available for both defence preparation courses (Navodaya, Sainik School, RIMC, Military School) as well as our foundation batches for Class 1–10. Call us to understand which one is right for your child.
What if my child's performance drops after getting the scholarship? ▼
We review scholarship students' performance every month. We work closely with them to ensure they stay on track. The scholarship is continued as long as the student attends regularly and shows genuine effort and commitment.
